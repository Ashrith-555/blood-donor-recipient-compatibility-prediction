Understanding the Machine Learning Blood Compatibility Matrix

Clinical Compatibility Factors

Our predictive Machine Learning models (Random Forest, Naïve Bayes, KNN, Decision Tree) rely on several critical medical variables to determine safe donor-recipient pairings. Below is an explanation of the core features evaluated by our algorithm.

1. RhD Factor Importance

The Rhesus (Rh) factor is an inherited protein found on the surface of red blood cells. Our ML models strictly penalize Rh-positive to Rh-negative donations to prevent severe hemolytic reactions.

2. Crossmatch Results

A serological crossmatch verifies compatibility. The algorithm ingests historical crossmatch success/failure rates to flag risky pairings that might otherwise appear compatible by basic ABO typing.

3. Transfusion History

Patients with multiple past transfusions often develop alloantibodies. Our Random Forest model heavily weights this feature, requiring stricter matching criteria for frequent recipients.

4. Disease Risk Factors

Donor disease history (e.g., infectious diseases, chronic conditions) is a primary elimination filter. The Naïve Bayes classifier calculates the probability of disease transmission based on systemic data.

Machine Learning in Hematology

Why use four different algorithms?

Random Forest

Highest overall accuracy. Handles non-linear clinical data efficiently by building multiple decision trees.

Naïve Bayes

Excellent for calculating independent probabilistic risks (like rare alloantibody presence).

KNN

Identifies "nearest neighbor" patient profiles to suggest donors who matched similar past cases.

Decision Tree

Provides a transparent, human-readable flowchart of the compatibility decision for doctor review.
